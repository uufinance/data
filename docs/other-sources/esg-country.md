# ESG, Climate & Sustainable Finance — Country-Level Data

Country-level and sovereign data on ESG performance, climate adaptation, and governance frameworks.

## Sovereign ESG & Governance

- <a href="https://esgdata.worldbank.org/" target="_blank">World Bank Sovereign ESG Data Portal</a> <span class="badge badge-csv">CSV</span> <span class="badge badge-global">Global</span> — 71+ ESG indicators across 217 economies spanning 60 years, covering all 17 Sustainable Development Goals across environment, social, and governance pillars. Includes interactive score builder, income adjustment tool, and peer comparisons. Bulk download available via the <a href="https://datacatalog.worldbank.org/search/dataset/0037651/Environment--Social-and-Governance-Data" target="_blank">Data Catalog</a>. CC BY 4.0 license.
- <a href="https://www.ecgi.global/publications/codes" target="_blank">ECGI Corporate Governance Codes</a> <span class="badge badge-global">Global</span> — Free database of corporate governance codes, principles, and reforms from dozens of countries, maintained by the European Corporate Governance Institute. Documents downloadable as PDF. Includes monitoring reports and impact studies where available.

## Climate Adaptation

- <a href="https://www.fao.org/faostat/en/#data/ET" target="_blank">FAOSTAT Temperature Change</a> <span class="badge badge-csv">CSV</span> <span class="badge badge-global">Global</span> — Annual surface temperature change by country relative to the 1951–1980 baseline, from the UN Food and Agriculture Organization. Covers 200+ countries and territories from 1961 to present, with breakdowns by meteorological divisions. Free bulk download as CSV via FAOSTAT. Also accessible in Python via the <a href="https://pypi.org/project/faostat/" target="_blank">`faostat`</a> package.

```bash
pip install faostat
```

- <a href="https://gain.nd.edu/our-work/country-index/download-data/" target="_blank">ND-GAIN Country Index</a> <span class="badge badge-csv">CSV</span> <span class="badge badge-global">Global</span> — Annual country-level scores measuring vulnerability to climate change and readiness to adapt, from the Notre Dame Global Adaptation Initiative. Covers 182 countries from 1995 to present. Vulnerability is assessed across six life-supporting sectors (food, water, health, ecosystem services, human habitat, and infrastructure); readiness captures economic, governance, and social capacity to leverage investments for adaptation. Data downloadable as CSV. Widely used in research on climate finance, sovereign risk, and ESG investing.
